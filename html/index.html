How to Prepare Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup

Welcome to this tutorial on how to prepare Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup

About Egusi Soup

Egusi soup is a popular Nigerian soup made with ground melon seeds and vegetables. It is usually served with pounded yam, a starchy side dish made from yam.

To make egusi soup, you will need:

  • Ground egusi seeds
  • Assorted meats (beef, goat meat, chicken, etc.)
  • Assorted vegetables (spinach, bitter leaf, pumpkin leaves, etc.)
  • Fish, crayfish
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Onions
  • Palm oil

To make the soup, heat the palm oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and fry until translucent. Add the ground egusi and fry for 5 minutes, stirring constantly. Add the chicken or beef stock and stir to combine. Add the cooked meat or fish, salt, crayfish, and pepper. Add seasoning cubes and salt. Cover and let simmer for 10 minutes. Add the spinach or pumpkin leaves and stir to combine. Let cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the leaves are wilted. Serve with pounded yam.

About Pounded Yam

Pounded yam is a staple food in many African countries, including Nigeria. It is made by boiling yam and then pounding it until it becomes a smooth, stretchy dough-like consistency. It is usually served with soups and stews, such as egusi soup.

To make pounded yam, you will need:

  • Yam
  • Water

To make the pounded yam, peel and cut the yam into small pieces. Put the yam pieces in a pot and add water to cover. Let cook until the yam is soft, about 15-20 minutes. Drain the water and put the yam in a mortar. Use a pestle to pound the yam until it becomes smooth and stretchy. Serve with egusi soup.
